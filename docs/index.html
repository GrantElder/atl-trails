Purpose

This is an individual platform for walking, exploring Atlanta, and journalling. Walking and journalling support work-life balance and mental well-being. Research shows that walking—especially in nature—reduces depressive and anxiety symptoms and improves mood and stress resilience.1 Journaling has been shown to improve mental distress and well-being, particularly for those managing elevated anxiety.2

Vision

  • Promote — Being outdoors, multiuse trails, urban exploring, Atlanta neighborhoods
  • Publish everywhere — GitHub and this website are the source of truth
  • Individual platform — Not designed to work with other people's ideas; personal exploration and documentation
  • Keep people updated — A following is okay, but that is not the whole purpose

Values

  • Minimalism — Simple tools, low friction
  • AI as a tool — Use AI to organize thoughts, plans, and tools; not to replace the walking or the writing
  • Plain data formats — Markdown, GPX, JSON; protocols and formats that last
  • Open source tools — Prefer tools that are transparent and community-maintained

References

1 Kelly P, et al. Walking on sunshine: scoping review of the evidence for walking and mental health. British Journal of Sports Medicine. 2018;52(12):800. bjsm.bmj.com

2 Baikie KA, Wilhelm K. Emotional and physical health benefits of expressive writing. Advances in Psychiatric Treatment. 2005. See also: Smyth JM, et al. Efficacy of journaling in the management of mental illness: a systematic review and meta-analysis. NCBI. ncbi.nlm.nih.gov
